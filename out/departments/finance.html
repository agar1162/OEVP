FAQFrequently Asked Questions

What is the CASSA Grant

The CASSA Grant (Campus Activity Support and Sponsorship Allocation) is a fund managed in collaboration with the ASUC Chief Financial Officer to support student-led programs, events, and initiatives. It provides financial resources to enrich student life at UC Berkeley.

Who can apply for the CASSA Grant?

Registered Student Organizations (RSOs) and other eligible campus groups or members can apply for the CASSA Grant to fund activities that benefit the student body. Specific eligibility criteria and deadlines are available on the ASUC website.

How can I apply for the CASSA Grant?

Applications for the CASSA Grant can be submitted through a form when the CASSA Fund Advisory Committee convenes during the school year. You will need to provide a detailed budget, a description of your program or event, and an explanation of how it aligns with ASUCâ€™s mission to enhance student life.

Can students provide input on how ASUC funds are allocated?

Yes! Students can share their feedback on ASUC funding priorities through surveys, open forums, or by contacting the Finance Department directly. Input from the campus community helps guide financial decision-making.

What types of financial resources does the ASUC provide for students?

Registered Student Organizations (RSOs), Campus-wide events and initiatives, Grants, and Support for long-term projects benefiting students
